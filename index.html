COVID - 19 Measures

COVID-19

COVID-19 is caused by severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus. The virus usually moves from one person to another with small drops made when coughing or sneezing. Coronavirus can also spread when people touch a surface with the virus, and then they touch their face.

1. Covid in lungs

Covid in lungs

COVID-19, the disease caused by the new coronavirus, can cause lung complications such as pneumonia and, in the most severe cases, acute respiratory distress syndrome, or ARDS. Sepsis, another possible complication of COVID-19, can also cause lasting harm to the lungs and other organs

2. Covid in Throat

Covid in Throat

From fever to an itchy cough and body pain, COVID-19 symptoms are way too similar to that of a cold or flu. However, experts have repeatedly said that the earliest symptoms a person has can alert them about an active infection, including a sore throat.

3. COVID-19 in Nose

Covid in nose

As more and more people have begun to step out of their homes, the chances of contracting the infection have also increased exponentially. While earlier breathlessness, fever and dry cough were considered the hallmarks of COVID-19, it is presenting itself in absolutely novel ways in different people. It is important to note that while runny nose and congestion are not typical symptoms of coronavirus, some patients do report them.

In any given moment we have two options: to step forward into growth or to step forward into safety

Prevention of COVID-19

  • Wash your hands frequently and carefully
  • Avoid touching your face
  • Don’t share personal items
  • Cover your mouth and nose when you cough and sneeze
  • Clean and disinfect surfaces
  • Take physical (social) distancing seriously
  • Do not gather in groups
  • Avoid eating or drinking in public places
  • Wear a mask

symptoms

Some of the major symptoms of COVID-19 are:

  • Itchy throat
  • Dry throat
  • Dry cough
  • High temperature
  • Shortness of breath
  • Loss of smell
